This section documents my cybersecurity learning, infrastructure monitoring research, Linux hardening notes, and experiments with practical defensive tooling.

Current areas of focus include endpoint visibility, secure deployment practices, access control, logging, and web3-adjacent infrastructure risk.

ls -t research/

Research Scope

The public lab is organized around four practical questions. What should be visible? Which controls reduce risk without making a system unusable? What evidence proves a change worked? What must stay private when the work is documented?

  • Endpoint visibilityLogs, events, and signals that support useful investigation.
  • Secure deploymentTrust boundaries, preflight checks, and repeatable release procedures.
  • Access and hardeningLeast privilege, configuration review, and Linux system hygiene — plus professional guidance on limiting and eliminating scope creep, where colleagues accumulate more access keys and permissions than their role requires, regardless of motive, ego or otherwise.
  • Safe evidenceRedacted screenshots, terminal excerpts, and notes that teach without exposing infrastructure.

Detailed procedures and evidence will be added after hostnames, usernames, addresses, repository details, and credentials are removed.
